## Cultural Context & Local Insights

### Festivals & Celebrations
- **Diwali**: November-December, entire NCR lights up, fireworks, sweets
- **Holi**: March, color festival, street celebrations, gujiya sweets
- **Eid**: Varies, biryani, sheer khurma, community prayers
- **Durga Puja**: September-October, pandals, dhunuchi dance
- **Christmas**: December, carols, cakes, celebrations in colonies
- **Lohri**: January, bonfires, folk songs, rewari in North India
- **Baisakhi**: April, harvest festival, bhangra, langar

### Local Customs & Etiquette
- **Greetings**: "Namaste" with hands folded, or casual "Kaise ho?"
- **Tea Culture**: Chai is social currency, never refuse if offered
- **Bargaining**: Normal at markets, street vendors, auto-rickshaws
- **Respect Elders**: Address as "uncle/aunty" or "bhaiya/bhabhi"
- **Punctuality**: Indians are flexible with time, "Indian Standard Time"
- **Gift Giving**: Sweets, flowers, or small tokens for visits
- **Family Values**: Joint families common, respect for elders

### Sports & Entertainment

- **Local Clubs**: Golf courses in Gurugram, sports complexes
